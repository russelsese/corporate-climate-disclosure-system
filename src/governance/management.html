Escalation Procedures
How climate-related issues are escalated to the board

Management escalates climate-related matters to the Board Audit & Risk Committee (BARC) according to the following thresholds and procedures:

  1. Immediate escalation (within 24 hours): Any climate-related event or development that could materially affect the company's financial position, reputation, or regulatory standing. The CFO and CSO jointly notify the BARC Chair.
  2. Quarterly escalation: All climate risk register updates, emissions performance against targets, and regulatory developments are presented at each BARC quarterly meeting. Responsibility: Risk Manager and CSO.
  3. Annual escalation: Full climate scenario analysis results, transition plan progress, and AASB S2 disclosure draft are presented for board approval. Responsibility: CFO with support from CSO and Company Secretary.
  4. Ad-hoc escalation: Material changes to climate policy, significant new physical risks identified, or failure to meet a disclosed target are escalated within 5 business days to the full Board.

AASB S2 §7(b) Guidance

§7(b) — Management Role
Disclose information about management's role in the governance of climate-related risks and opportunities.
Mandatory
Required disclosures include:
  • Whether dedicated management positions or committees have been assigned responsibility for climate-related risks and opportunities
  • How management reports to the governance body
  • The organisational position or committee and how it relates to other functions
  • Controls and procedures for managing climate-related risks
Escalation Procedures
Entities must describe how and when climate issues are escalated to the board, including thresholds and frequency.
