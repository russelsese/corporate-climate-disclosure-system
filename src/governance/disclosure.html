DISCLOSURE NARRATIVE
§7(a) Board oversight of climate-related risks and opportunities

The Board's Audit & Risk Committee (BARC) has primary oversight responsibility for climate-related risks and opportunities at AusMetal Holdings Pty Ltd. BARC meets quarterly and reviews the climate risk register at each meeting. The full Board receives an annual climate briefing and approves the Climate Transition Plan. BARC is chaired by Jennifer Walsh (independent director) and comprises four members with relevant financial and operational expertise.

§7(b) Management's role in assessing and managing climate risks

The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) has executive accountability for climate-related financial risk. The Chief Sustainability Officer (CSO) manages the sustainability programme and reports to the CFO. The ESG Manager coordinates data collection, disclosure preparation, and regulatory compliance. Climate risk is integrated into the Enterprise Risk Management framework, with escalation to BARC for risks rated HIGH or CRITICAL.

§8 Executive remuneration linked to climate performance

From FY2024, short-term incentive (STI) arrangements for the CEO and CFO include climate-related performance metrics. The CEO's STI includes a 10% weighting linked to achievement of Scope 1 & 2 emission reduction targets. The CFO's STI includes a 5% weighting linked to TCFD/AASB S2 compliance readiness. The CSO's STI includes a 25% weighting linked to key sustainability programme milestones.

§9 How governance body oversees the Climate Transition Plan

The Board reviews and approves the Climate Transition Plan (CTP) annually. The CTP was most recently updated in December 2025 and approved at the December 2025 Full Board meeting. Progress against CTP milestones is reported to BARC at each quarterly meeting. Any material changes to the CTP require full Board approval.
